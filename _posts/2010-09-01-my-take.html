First up: Apple and the 4g phone “leak”

I think it was deliberate, although part of me is pretty sure it wasn’t. Not because Apple needs the press, but partly because it comes in a fairly vicious time for Apple and developers after the 3.3.1 clause in the SDK EULA. Maybe something to keep the general public happy and interested. So for what it’s worth, Apple, keep your stuff under control. Those kinda shenanigans melt servers with blog posts, feeds, and basically clog the interwebs. Just.Don’t.Do.It.

Better yet, what if this is another Gizmodo hoax? Faux-iPhone from China maybe? Some kickass design work from the graphics design department?

Next: Gizmodo

Companies or services (or whatever you call a group like the one behind Gizmodo) run on a business model that revolves solely around numbers. They’re really in the business of creating content around their ads. Ads is really the major way a blogging or news service makes its revenue, especially on the web with a non-subscriber based model. So it makes sense they would go after the biggest news story possible. I can’t blame them. Sure they probably could have done it better, sure they probably screwed Powell over.

The way I see it though, any company driven by “followers” rather than the quality of the services they can provide is already losing. What’s the point? Tabloidism is a dumb way to make a living. I’m not even sure I’d call what Gizmodo does a “service”. Its putting out content. Really low quality (and probably unedited) content… What’s more is when they find a story like this they talk it to death. Somebody kill it already.

The point is, if we lose site of service quality for numbers, we end up like McDonalds: Everywhere serving zillions but its not a place you’re proud to work. Its not something you’re proud to say you participated in.

I do have to say, the hardliner Apple geeks love ripping on Gizzie and its really funny to see. Settle down yo.
