The following are my notes from a presentation given by Jan Schultink, CEO of Idea Transplant. He came and gave a talk as part of our SalesSchool program to give relevant content to sales people, in particular for this evening, those who are seeking VC funding.

Also note, we have another event tonight, again at NYU. More great material focused around much of the same ideas, but more geared towards sales in general, rather than VC pitches.

The video will be up on the site some point in the near future, but for now, here’s my take on the presentation.

How to Design Stunning Sales Presentations

Images

No bullet points: means you must construct, audience is faster at reading then you will be at explaining through points.

instead…

Images are emotional shortcuts to make points.
Packages information much faster. Less need to explain.
Brings emotion to connect to information and boost memory.

Daunting images for backgrounds will burn visual trademark to the information.
Gives big memory recall triggers.

source: iStockPhoto.
warning: easy to find a person who is duplicated many times. be unique.
develop an eye for picking.

source: flickr CC
warning: quality. be sure to use creative commons, w/ attribution.

Watch proportions.
Ban clip art.
Use advertisements as examples or tutorials for presentations. Good/valuable trends show up here.

Leave much whitespace.
No slide limit, use a time limit instead.

symmetry is bad. use the rule of thirds.

align. poor alignment distracts.

Sequence - momentup.

Start left and lower, up and to the right gives a feeling of energy and positive movement.

Easy on the animations. Keeps it simple and professional.

Numbers: simplify and round. No need for hyper-accuracy.

Charts. Don’t do small axis labels, don’t need 3D graphics. Simplify, show single trends.

Use blank slides in order to bring attention back to presenter.

Hygiene - general look and feel.

No comic sans.
No odd colors.
Use professional email addresses with company domain name

Colors: Adobe Kuler.
Be consistent across presentation. Same colors for all slides.

Give content space. Don’t do a lot of repeating elements.
Use colors that fit with your corporate identity.

Stories.

Do not use an agenda slide. Repetition does not help. Only gives a feeling of being forced.

Presentations are not references. Not business plan driven. Its more driven by trying to find holes.

Using very short “stories”/information snippets will cause others to want the information, to start to try to find and fill in the details.

Mind memorizes by placing information in spaces (like a picture of a whiteboard)

Think product placement within a story.

Don’t look at powerpoint, use a sheet of paper or listen to yourself to build a presentation.
Will give you a roadmap to build on and find the important points.

Take time to build presentation. 2 weeks for development + 2-3 days to build slides.

Content - for the VC

Team intro is helpful at beginning, doesn’t have to be on slides.

Presentation is not just the slides. Also many other inputs. Such as:

Gut conviction + Head conviction.
Must pull and convince both.

YOU. You carry the deck along. The body language speaks.

Convince me that:

  • People will buy
  • You can sell it

Build trust through honesty.
No: “traffic is thru the roof” -or- “we have no competitors”
Feel free to voice uncertainty, with conviction you are still right.

Types of conversations:

  • 10 second conf chat
    • Use sentences, not massive amounts of buzzwords.
    • Made to Stick (book)
    • Don’t leave people with just a random tune stuck in their heads.
    • Mission statements have no bearing on those who have no context to work in
    • Go with specific issues that may just be a single part of the picture
  • 30 minute pitch
    • TED is 20 minutes.
    • No need to fill all the time.
    • Be concise and use remaining time for dialog
    • Don’t preach to the converted. Ideas already taking off don’t need to be given more value.
    • Don’t slap all your ideas together into a frankenstein deck.
    • Pick up signals presentees are sending as responses to your info
      • Address “elephant in the room issues” early to remove distractions.
      • Will help focus the investor.
    • Focus on: What are you doing currently? Even if its a rougher overall picture.
    • Pitch the problem rather than the features of the solution.
    • Use visuals to show problems so that the solution becomes apparent by itself.
    • Maintain momentum. Demo of products will kill this more than anything.
      • Maybe use screenshots instead.
      • Let them play afterwards.
    • “If you can’t explain it to a 6 year old, you don’t understand it yourself” - Einstein
    • Show very short term plan in great detail. Show long term in more ballpark terms.
    • Go simpler the greater the lengths of time
    • Actually justify long term numbers. Plan.
    • Be consistent.
    • Framework structures are boring. Recreate as your own using basic/simple diagrams.
    • Highlight a complimentary team that works together well.
  • 15 minute version
    • Print out, don’t staple. Easy to move around within a presentation. Use paper. More flexible
  • Board Meeting
    • will not depend entirely on deck
    • move off to excel or detailed data transmission tools.

For potential “pre deck screenings” either give information as part of the slide, write out the message in a side column, or create two decks.

Webinars

Get technology set up correctly
Page reference for fast switching (easy to go back or move around within deck)
Avoid 5 second charts. Charts need to be focus points.
Make it about fewer slide transitions.
Build up with pointers.
Explain why you are silent (drinking water, etc).

Learn

Presentation Zen - Garr Reynolds
Brain Rules - John Medina

Meeting notes in public domain:
Mark Suster, Fred Wilson
Gain insight into successful presentations. Some VCs give pointers to what they liked.

One more thing.

Practice!!! Rumor has it, Steve Jobs takes 3 working days to practice.
Must be on top of/know totality of content in order to be spontaneous.

Always make point early. Don’t get cut off.

What slide stays on the longest? The last one. Usually says “Q&A”…
Should be something that you discussed to help burn into minds as they sit doing something else.
