Couple of posts upcoming.

First one will be on a mini-app I’m working on for Hargray. Its just kinda a shout-out to a cool little helper for Textile forms. Hargray peeps needed a quick way to do email blasts, since the Oracle Forms stuff doesn’t give a slick/easy way to format emails. So I got the request to build a simple form for emailing stuff to customers.

Well I’ve become a big fan of Textile. I use it right here for formatting my blog entries right here. Its just a super slick way to format text without the craziness of HTML markup. Plus its a little more intuitive for non-technical users. At first I figured, hey, I’ll just point email creators at the textile formatting guide and they can do all the markup by hand. But lo and behold, I found felttippens Textile-Editor-Helper. It adds a nifty little editor bar with a lot of the markup tools you’d expect to see so that you can simply press the button and it generates the markup for you.

Then I wanted to give the users a way to preview the actual email content before sending, so I created a little helper that does the following

  $("#render_preview").click(function (e) {

    e.preventDefault();

    $('.preview').load("preview", {text:$("#email_blast_message").val()});  

  });

:textile

  #{@text}

  def translate

    @text = params[:text]

    render "shared/translate"

  end

Which is a super fast way of ajaxing the text-area text to an ActionController::Metal action, which faster than thought possible, returns the HTML output version of the Textile markup’d text! Its incredibly fast since I’m only using Metal rather than a full ActionController object. Plus its very simple in what it does, so I’m pretty happy with how it worked out.
